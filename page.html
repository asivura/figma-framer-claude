Your questions answered

Common Questions

Have questions? We've got answers. Everything you need to know about your LiveHealthy visits with HealthTap through Clover Health.

How much does an online video visit cost a Clover Health member?

Eligible Clover Health members will have no visit fees on HealthTap.

How long do I have to wait for a visit?

Depending on the schedule of the HealthTap doctor you choose, you may be able to start your virtual visit with the doctor as soon as 30 minutes after scheduling your appointment.

What if I forget to ask all my questions during my visit?

You can follow up with the primary care doctor by text, anytime. At no cost to you. Just select the "Text - Free" option under the doctor's name in your HealthTap account. If your doctor needs to see you in another video visit to address your questions, scheduling your next video visit is easy.

How can I learn more about my Clover Health rewards?

Visit cloverhealth.com/livehealthy. You'll find information on how to redeem your rewards, check your balance, and who to call at Clover Health if you have questions.

Can an online doctor write prescriptions?

Yes! Get same-day prescriptions and refills sent to your preferred retail pharmacy or to Clover Health's preferred mail-order pharmacy — CVS Caremark — within 2-hours. HealthTap doctors cannot prescribe medications that are restricted at the federal or state level — such as DEA scheduled medications — or some medications that are inappropriate for prescription in video visits.

How do I select my preferred pharmacy?

Before your first virtual visit on HealthTap, you will be asked to select your preferred local retail pharmacy. HealthTap also knows your preferred mail-order pharmacy, CVS Caremark. You will have the opportunity to update your preferred local retail pharmacy every time you schedule a virtual appointment. Additionally, you may change your preferred local retail pharmacy anytime in your HealthTap account "Records".

What are the benefits of having a primary care doctor on HealthTap?

On HealthTap, you can choose a primary care doctor who will be committed to providing you with high-quality, personalized care. HealthTap doctors take the time to listen and understand you so they can provide you with great care based on your specific needs. Because HealthTap is an online clinic, you'll be able to talk to your HealthTap primary care doctor using your smartphone, tablet, or computer and won't need to travel anywhere to see your HealthTap doctor.

Do HealthTap doctors provide the same level of care as in-office doctors?

HealthTap primary care doctors are able to provide a broad spectrum of care in video visits. However, some health issues require in-person care, such as with a specialist or with an in-person primary care doctor. When you talk to your doctor about a health issue, they will let you know if in-person care is appropriate and will provide guidance on how to get the level of care you need.

How long do primary care video appointments take?

Appointments will take an average of 30 minutes.

How are labs/blood pressure handled?

In a HealthTap virtual visit, the doctor can order lab tests that run on blood, urine, and stool samples. When a doctor orders a lab test, HealthTap sends the order electronically to Quest Diagnostics and you can fulfill it at a Quest Diagnostics patient service center. You will also receive a copy of the lab order, which you can take to another laboratory service, such as LabCorp.

If you get your lab tests at a laboratory other than Quest Diagnostics, ask the laboratory to fax the results to HealthTap's clinical fax (650-618-1445), or you may upload a copy of the results to your Health Files folder in your chart and message your doctor to inform them the results are ready for review.

HealthTap doctors cannot order lab tests with sample types that cannot be collected at a patient service center (such as throat, nose, or genitourinary swabs). Your doctor may refer you to in-person care when such lab tests are needed.

If you have an at-home blood pressure device, you may take readings and share them with your doctor in a virtual visit or private message. Enter your readings into the "Health Metrics" tab in your chart. If you don't have a device at home, your doctor may recommend visiting a pharmacy or clinic for a blood pressure measurement.
